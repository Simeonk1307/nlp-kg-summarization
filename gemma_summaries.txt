Ovarian Hyperstimulation Syndrome (OHSS) is a serious IVF complication (1-10% incidence) 
driven by VEGF-induced capillary permeability, causing ascites and potentially life-threatening 
conditions. While severe OHSS traditionally requires inpatient care, this study evaluates pigtail catheter 
drainage for outpatient management. In a retrospective study of 33 patients with severe/critical OHSS, 88% were 
successfully managed as outpatients. By combining pigtail catheter drainage, intravenous fluid replacement (using hespan), 
and diligent monitoring, most patients achieved rapid symptom relief and faster recovery. Only 12% required hospitalization, primarily 
due to severe pleural effusion. The study concludes that pigtail catheter drainage is a safe and efficient alternative for managing many 
severe OHSS cases outside the hospital. However, clinicians must remain vigilant, as rapid clinical deterioration can occur, necessitating 
immediate hospital admission for critical patients.

OHSS is a serious complication of ovulation induction, often manifesting as ascites. This study evaluates the use of a pigtail catheter 
(a type of catheter) for the outpatient management of severe OHSS patients, categorized by the Golan classification. Using a GIFT (gamete intra-fallopian treatment) 
trocar and cannula for trans-abdominal insertion, researchers found that 30 of 33 patients were managed safely as outpatients. Treatment involved rehydration with 
crystalloids and Hespan (a prophylactic) instead of albumin (a total protein). While most patients recovered at home, some required admission to an inpatient setting 
or an intensive care unit due to complications like severe pleural effusion or pulmonary embolism (a thromboembolic condition). The study concludes that pigtail catheter drainage is a safe, 
effective alternative to repeated paracentesis, though critical cases still necessitate intensive hospital management to avoid mortality.
